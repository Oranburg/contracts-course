Tome of Law: R2d § 177 (Undue Influence)

"Undue influence is unfair persuasion of a party who is under the domination of the person exercising the persuasion or who... is justified in assuming that that person will not act in a manner inconsistent with his welfare."

Unlike duress, which requires an improper threat, undue influence focuses on the exploitation of a special relationship of trust or dominance to overcome a victim's independent judgment.
