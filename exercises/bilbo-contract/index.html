Contract of Engagement for Burglarious Services

Dated as of the Twenty-Seventh Day of April, in the Year of Shire Reckoning 1341

This Contract of Engagement for Burglarious Services (the "Agreement") is entered into by and between Thorin Oakenshield, acting for himself and purporting to act on behalf of the Company defined below, and Bilbo Baggins of Bag End, Hobbiton, in the Shire.

Recitals

Whereas Thorin Oakenshield and a company of thirteen dwarves are engaged in an expedition to recover treasure, heirlooms, and other property alleged to belong to them by inheritance, memory, grievance, or ancient right;

Whereas Bilbo Baggins has been proposed, chiefly by a wizard, as a suitable person to serve as burglar, scout, and finder-out of hidden matters, despite limited prior experience in the trade;

Whereas the parties desire to set forth in writing the terms upon which such services shall be rendered, compensated, disputed, excused, and otherwise regretted;

Article 1. Definitions

1.1 "Company"

"Company" means Thorin Oakenshield and the twelve dwarves traveling with him: Balin, Dwalin, Fili, Kili, Dori, Nori, Ori, Oin, Gloin, Bifur, Bofur, and Bombur.

1.2 "Burglar"

"Burglar" means Bilbo Baggins, in his capacity as independent provider of burglarious, scouting, stealth, negotiating, and related services.

1.3 "Expedition"

"Expedition" means the journey to the Lonely Mountain for recovering treasure and heirlooms therefrom.

1.4 "Treasure"

Coin, plate, jewels, trade goods, heirlooms, weapons, works of art, portable valuables — but excludes personal clothing, ordinary travel gear, consumed provisions, and sentimental complaints not readily convertible into money.

1.5 "Net Proceeds"

Treasure actually recovered and reduced to possession, less only expenses that are reasonable, documented, actually incurred, and directly related to the Expedition.

1.6 "Material Breach"

A breach that substantially defeats the purpose of this Agreement, including willful betrayal, unjustified abandonment, deliberate concealment of material danger, or wrongful refusal to pay the Burglar's earned share.

Article 2. Engagement and Status

2.1 The Company engages the Burglar as its exclusive burglar, scout, finder of secret doors, observer of danger, occasional negotiator, and general provider of improbable good fortune in narrow circumstances.

2.2 The Burglar accepts, reserving always the right to grumble privately, long for his armchair, and object to adventures in principle while performing them in practice.

2.3 The Burglar is an independent contractor — not a servant, partner, joint venturer, or co-sovereign. Nothing shall require the Burglar to grow a beard, sing in company, or adopt dwarvish views regarding punctual revenge.

2.4 The Burglar does not warrant or guarantee success, silence, safe return, solvency, defeat of any dragon, or emotional stability within the Company.

Article 5. Compensation

5.1 The Burglar shall receive one-fourteenth (1/14) of the Net Proceeds.

5.2 Payment within a reasonable time after appraisal and division, accounting for safety, transport, and the temporary disorder that often follows dragons.

5.3 Payment in coin, jewels, plate, or negotiable valuables — but not items that are cursed, notoriously stolen beyond the scope of the Expedition, too heavy for a hobbit, or likely to attract a lawsuit on the road home.

5.4 No Discretionary Dilution. No deduction merely because a dwarf claims an expense was glorious, traditional, morale-enhancing, or worth every copper at the time.

5.5 Books and Inspection Rights. The Burglar may inspect the accounting. Ambiguity construed against the party who kept the books.

Article 7. Obligations of the Company

7.1 Support. Provide cooperation, information, access, and assistance reasonably necessary for the Burglar to perform.

7.2 Disclosure. Disclose all known extraordinary dangers: dragons, blood-feuds, curses, disputed title claims, prior failed expeditions, unreliable maps, missing keys, and any tendency by Thorin to become unreasonable in the presence of treasure.

7.3 Equalization of Risk. No plainly suicidal task unless the Company members who insist accept substantially similar danger.

7.4 Authority and Ratification. Any dwarf who knowingly accepts the Burglar's benefits has ratified this Agreement.

Article 9. Risk Allocation and Excuses

9.1 Assumption of Risk. The Burglar acknowledges hazards including fire, claws, teeth, blades, poison, imprisonment, hunger, weather, sorcery, and group singing at unsafe volumes.

9.2 Misrepresentation. Assumption of risk shall not bar relief where the Company materially misrepresented, half-truthed, recklessly omitted, or unduly cheerfully assured.

9.3 Mistake. Mutual mistake as to a basic assumption may allow rescission or reformation.

9.4 Frustration. Supervening events destroying the principal purpose may discharge remaining duties.

9.5 Impossibility. Excused where objectively impossible by dragon-fire, enchantment, or loss of access.

9.6 Force Majeure. Capture by trolls, goblins, spiders, or elves; pursuit by wargs; intervention by eagles; dragon attacks; battles involving multiple armies.

9.7 Duty to Resume. Resume when able; use good-faith efforts to reduce avoidable delay.

Article 11. Good Faith and Special Covenants

11.1 Good Faith. The parties shall act in good faith and deal fairly.

11.2 No Secret Redefinition. The Company shall not redefine Net Proceeds after Treasure is found, reclassify Treasure as morale, or characterize divisible wealth as sentimental rubble.

11.3 No Opportunistic Concealment. The Burglar shall not conceal substantial recoveries for coercing renegotiation, except to the limited extent temporary concealment is reasonably necessary to prevent immediate violence or mutual ruin.

11.4 Confidential Information. No disclosure of routes, secret entrances, keys, maps, passwords, or treasure intelligence except where reasonably necessary to save life, secure lawful assistance, or prevent a greater wrong.

Article 13. Remedies

13.1 General. All remedies at law or equity: damages, restitution, specific relief, declaratory relief.

13.2 Liquidated Damages for Wrongful Exclusion. Greater of: (a) 1/14 of fair appraised value, or (b) three times unreimbursed expenditures. Intended as a reasonable pre-estimate, not a penalty, however emotionally deserved a penalty might be.

13.3 Mitigation. Reasonable efforts to mitigate, but no party required to mitigate by marching unarmed into a dragon's mouth.

Article 15. General Provisions

15.1 Entire Agreement. Supersedes all prior oral or written discussions, songs, boasts, assurances, or strategically vague wizardly remarks.

15.2 Amendments. Written and signed, except oral modifications in mortal peril may be enforceable if fully performed and proven by disinterested witnesses.

15.3 Interpretation. Ambiguities construed against the drafter.

15.5 Governing Law. Customs of the Shire, supplemented by the common law of the Free Peoples and equitable principles suitable to civilized folk.

15.6 Dispute Resolution. Mediation first, then binding arbitration: one hobbit, one dwarf, and one neutral elf, each selected by fair means and with some reluctance.

15.7 Venue. Reasonably accessible, substantially defensible, and to the extent practicable, free from dragons.

15.10 Survival. Payment, indemnity, restitution, reliance, remedies, confidentiality, dispute resolution, and interpretation survive termination.

THORIN OAKENSHIELD
For Himself and the Company

BILBO BAGGINS
The Burglar

GANDALF THE GREY
Wizard, Recruiter, and Person Unhelpfully Certain It Will All Work Out
