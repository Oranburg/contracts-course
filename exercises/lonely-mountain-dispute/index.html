Student case file

Facts

Case narrative

The Company reaches the Lonely Mountain. Smaug dies, and the treasure becomes recoverable. Bilbo secretly takes the Arkenstone and transfers it to Bard and the Elvenking in an attempt to force a peaceful settlement and avert bloodshed. Thorin responds by accusing Bilbo of betrayal and refusing to honor Bilbo's claim on Bilbo's preferred terms. The dispute hardens, the Battle of the Five Armies intervenes, and the parties later reconcile only imperfectly.

Assumptions for the exercise
  • Assume the Burglar Services Contract is valid unless one side proves otherwise.
  • Assume the Arkenstone is unique and difficult to value.
  • Assume the treasure is vast but not perfectly liquid.
  • Assume both sides are now negotiating after the battle with adjudication as the backup.
Questions students must answer
  • Did Bilbo materially breach by taking and transferring the Arkenstone?
  • Did the Company materially breach by withholding Bilbo's share?
  • Which remedy or combination of remedies best serves each client?
  • How does the uniqueness of the Arkenstone affect the analysis?
  • What should good faith, mitigation, and unjust enrichment do here?
Confidential role rooms

Choose a side

Theme

Value conferred, then denied

Your client rendered dangerous and indispensable services. Even if Bilbo acted irregularly, the Company cannot erase the benefit it received.

Weakness to manage

Secret transfer

Bilbo took and transferred a unique asset without Thorin's permission, which the other side will frame as material breach and unauthorized self-help.

A
Wrongful withholding.
The strongest contract theory is that the Company cannot deny compensation after performance has substantially succeeded.
B
Restitution fallback.
Even if Bilbo breached, he conferred measurable value and should not leave empty-handed.
C
Good-faith leverage.
Bilbo can argue he used the Arkenstone to avert war rather than to loot secretly for selfish profit.
Theme

Authority and betrayal

Your client argues that Bilbo exceeded his authority and impaired the Company's bargaining position by transferring the Arkenstone to outsiders.

Weakness to manage

Bad-faith optics

Thorin's refusal to compromise and his treatment of Bilbo make the Company vulnerable on good faith, opportunism, and unjust enrichment.

A
Material breach.
The Arkenstone is unique, and Bilbo had no unilateral right to seize and transfer it.
B
Limits on self-help.
Even if Bilbo was owed money, he could not choose the most symbolically important jewel on his own terms.
C
Valuation uncertainty.
The one-fourteenth share may be hard to compute, making expectation damages less obvious than Bilbo claims.

Questions worth pressing in class

1
Why is expectation not obviously right?
Push students on valuation difficulty, uniqueness, timing, and uncertainty in division.
2
When does restitution outperform expectation?
Ask whether a breaching or possibly breaching party can still recover for substantial value conferred.
3
How much should good faith matter?
Force students to decide whether Bilbo's secrecy or Thorin's refusal is more remedially significant.
