# Contract of Engagement for Burglarious Services

**Dated as of the Twenty-Seventh Day of April, in the Year of Shire Reckoning 1341**

This Contract of Engagement for Burglarious Services (the "**Agreement**") is entered into by and between **Thorin Oakenshield**, acting for himself and purporting to act on behalf of the Company defined below, and **Bilbo Baggins** of Bag End, Hobbiton, in the Shire.

## Recitals

Whereas Thorin Oakenshield and a company of thirteen dwarves are engaged in an expedition to recover treasure, heirlooms, and other property alleged to belong to them by inheritance, memory, grievance, or ancient right;

Whereas Bilbo Baggins has been proposed, chiefly by a wizard, as a suitable person to serve as burglar, scout, and finder-out of hidden matters, despite limited prior experience in the trade;

Whereas the parties desire to set forth in writing the terms upon which such services shall be rendered, compensated, disputed, excused, and otherwise regretted;

Now, therefore, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein contained, the parties agree as follows.

## Article 1. Definitions

### 1.1 "Company"
"**Company**" means Thorin Oakenshield and the twelve dwarves traveling with him in connection with the expedition to the Lonely Mountain, namely: Balin, Dwalin, Fili, Kili, Dori, Nori, Ori, Oin, Gloin, Bifur, Bofur, and Bombur.

### 1.2 "Burglar"
"**Burglar**" means Bilbo Baggins, in his capacity as independent provider of burglarious, scouting, stealth, negotiating, and related services.

### 1.3 "Expedition"
"**Expedition**" means the journey undertaken by the Company for the purpose of reaching the Lonely Mountain, entering the same or its approaches, and recovering treasure and heirlooms therefrom.

### 1.4 "Treasure"
"**Treasure**" means coin, plate, jewels, trade goods, heirlooms, weapons, works of art, portable valuables, and other property of material value recovered by the Company in connection with the Expedition, but excludes personal clothing, ordinary travel gear, consumed provisions, and sentimental complaints not readily convertible into money.

### 1.5 "Net Proceeds"
"**Net Proceeds**" means the value of Treasure actually recovered and reduced to possession, less only those expenses that are reasonable, documented, actually incurred, and directly related to the Expedition.

### 1.6 "Material Breach"
"**Material Breach**" means a breach that substantially defeats the purpose of this Agreement, including willful betrayal, unjustified abandonment, deliberate concealment of material danger, or wrongful refusal to pay the Burglar's earned share.

## Article 2. Engagement and Status

### 2.1 Engagement
The Company hereby engages the Burglar as its exclusive burglar, scout, finder of secret doors, observer of danger, occasional negotiator, and general provider of improbable good fortune in narrow circumstances.

### 2.2 Acceptance
The Burglar accepts such engagement, reserving always the right to grumble privately, long for his armchair, and object to adventures in principle while performing them in practice.

### 2.3 Independent Contractor
The Burglar is engaged as an independent contractor and not as a servant, partner, joint venturer, or co-sovereign of the Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to require the Burglar to grow a beard, sing in company, or adopt dwarvish views regarding punctual revenge.

### 2.4 No Guarantee of Outcome
The Burglar does not warrant or guarantee success, silence, safe return, solvency of the Expedition, defeat of any dragon, or emotional stability within the Company.

## Article 3. Scope of Services

### 3.1 Services
The Burglar shall use reasonable efforts, skill, caution, and discretion to:

(a) inspect approaches, entrances, tunnels, doors, and chambers relevant to the Expedition;
(b) identify hazards, guards, traps, hostile creatures, and suspicious magical objects;
(c) enter places where discreet entry is reasonably required;
(d) retrieve or assist in retrieving Treasure where practicable; and
(e) communicate useful intelligence to the Company in time to avert disaster where possible.

### 3.2 Standard of Performance
The Burglar shall perform the Services with the care that a reasonably prudent hobbit of unusual courage and improving judgment would exercise under similar circumstances.

### 3.3 Excluded Services
The Burglar shall not be required, absent a separate written amendment, to perform as principal dragon-slayer, siege engineer, beast of burden, unpaid valet, or sacrificial distraction.

## Article 4. Term

### 4.1 Commencement
This Agreement shall commence upon execution.

### 4.2 Expiration
This Agreement shall continue until the earliest of the following:

(a) completion of the Expedition;
(b) final division of recoverable Treasure;
(c) lawful termination under Article 12;
(d) objective impossibility of further performance; or
(e) permanent demise of the Burglar, if accompanied by circumstances rendering further service impracticable even by heroic standards.

## Article 5. Compensation

### 5.1 Share of Net Proceeds
As consideration for the Services, the Burglar shall be entitled to **one-fourteenth (1/14)** of the Net Proceeds.

### 5.2 Payment Timing
The Burglar's share shall be calculated and tendered within a reasonable time after the Treasure has been appraised, reduced to possession, and made capable of fair division, taking account of safety, transport, and the temporary disorder that often follows dragons.

### 5.3 Form of Payment
Payment may be made in coin, jewels, plate, negotiable valuables, or other property of substantially equivalent appraised value, provided that the Burglar shall not be compelled to accept items that are cursed, notoriously stolen beyond the scope of the Expedition, too heavy for a hobbit to move, or likely to attract a lawsuit on the road home.

### 5.4 No Discretionary Dilution
No deduction shall be taken merely because a dwarf claims an expense was glorious, traditional, morale-enhancing, or worth every copper at the time.

### 5.5 Books and Inspection Rights
Upon reasonable request, the Burglar shall be entitled to inspect a fair accounting of Treasure recovered, expenses deducted, and valuations used in calculating Net Proceeds. Any ambiguity in the Company's accounts shall be construed against the party who kept them.

## Article 6. Expenses and Reimbursement

### 6.1 Company Expenses
The Company shall bear all ordinary and reasonable costs of the Burglar's travel, board, lodging, transport, rope, tools reasonably required, provisions, mending, and emergency treatment.

### 6.2 Advances by Burglar
If the Burglar reasonably advances funds or property to preserve the Expedition, rescue members of the Company, procure supplies, prevent catastrophe, secure release from captivity, or preserve recoverable Treasure, the Burglar shall be reimbursed before division of Net Proceeds.

### 6.3 Emergency Expenditures
Reimbursement shall not be denied solely because an expenditure was made in haste, fear, darkness, under pursuit, while invisible, or during an otherwise inconvenient apocalypse.

## Article 7. Obligations of the Company

### 7.1 Support
The Company shall provide such material cooperation, information, access, and assistance as are reasonably necessary for the Burglar to perform.

### 7.2 Disclosure of Known Risks
The Company shall disclose all known extraordinary dangers material to performance, including dragons, blood-feuds, curses, disputed claims of title, prior failed expeditions, unreliable maps, missing keys, and any tendency by Thorin to become unreasonable in the presence of treasure.

### 7.3 Equalization of Extreme Risk
The Company shall not require the Burglar to undertake a plainly suicidal task unless no reasonable alternative exists and the members of the Company who insist upon such task accept substantially similar exposure to danger.

### 7.4 Authority and Ratification
Thorin represents that he has authority to enter into this Agreement on behalf of the Company. Any member of the Company who knowingly accepts the benefits of the Burglar's performance shall be deemed to have ratified this Agreement and may not thereafter deny its binding force.

## Article 8. Conditions Precedent

### 8.1 Conditions to Direct Entry
The Burglar's duty to attempt direct entry into the dragon's lair or principal treasure chamber shall arise only if:

(a) the Company has made reasonable efforts to reach the relevant site;
(b) the Company has provided available maps, keys, instructions, and material intelligence;
(c) the entrance sought is located or reasonably locatable; and
(d) the Burglar has a fair opportunity to inspect the means of entry.

### 8.2 Effect of Failure
Failure of any condition precedent shall suspend the corresponding duty of the Burglar until such condition is satisfied, waived in writing, or excused by law.

### 8.3 No Constructive Satisfaction by Boasting
A condition precedent shall not be deemed satisfied merely because a wizard is confident, Thorin is impatient, or several dwarves assert that matters will sort themselves out.

## Article 9. Risk Allocation and Excuses

### 9.1 Assumption of Risk
The Burglar acknowledges that the Expedition involves serious hazards, including fire, claws, teeth, blades, poison, imprisonment, hunger, weather, sorcery, and group singing at unsafe volumes.

### 9.2 Misrepresentation
Assumption of risk shall not bar relief to the extent the Burglar relied upon a material misrepresentation, half-truth, reckless omission, or unduly cheerful assurance made by the Company or its recruiters.

### 9.3 Mistake
If, at the time of contracting, both parties were mistaken as to a basic assumption materially affecting the agreed exchange, the Agreement may be rescinded or reformed on just terms.

### 9.4 Frustration of Purpose
If a supervening event substantially destroys the principal purpose of this Agreement without fault of the party seeking relief, remaining duties may be discharged to the extent justice requires.

### 9.5 Impossibility and Impracticability
Performance shall be excused where it becomes objectively impossible or commercially impracticable by reason of events beyond a party's reasonable control, including earthquake, collapse, war, dragon-fire, enchantment, or loss of indispensable means of access.

### 9.6 Force Majeure
Neither party shall be liable for delay or failure in performance caused by events beyond reasonable control, including capture by trolls, goblins, spiders, or elves; pursuit by wargs; intervention by eagles; dragon attacks; battles involving multiple armies; or comparable calamities.

### 9.7 Duty to Resume
A party invoking this Article shall resume performance when reasonably able to do so and shall use good-faith efforts to reduce avoidable delay.

## Article 10. Indemnity, Restitution, and Reliance

### 10.1 Company Indemnity
The Company shall indemnify and hold harmless the Burglar from claims, losses, or injuries arising from the Company's material nondisclosure, abandonment, or wrongful acts within the Expedition.

### 10.2 Burglar Indemnity
The Burglar shall indemnify the Company only for losses caused by the Burglar's fraud, willful misconduct, or gross negligence.

### 10.3 Restitution
If this Agreement is terminated after the Burglar has conferred a measurable benefit on the Company, the Burglar shall be entitled to restitution in an amount necessary to avoid unjust enrichment.

### 10.4 Reliance
If the Agreement is avoided, rescinded, or discharged after the Burglar has reasonably changed position in reliance upon it, the Burglar shall be entitled to recover reliance damages to the extent necessary to place him in no worse position than if the Agreement had not been made.

### 10.5 Funeral and Heirs
If the Burglar dies in the course of performance, the Company shall pay reasonable funeral or memorial expenses as circumstances permit and shall remain liable to the Burglar's heirs or estate for any accrued reimbursement, restitution, or earned compensation.

## Article 11. Good Faith and Special Covenants

### 11.1 Good Faith
The parties shall act in good faith and deal fairly with one another.

### 11.2 No Secret Redefinition
The Company shall not redefine Net Proceeds after Treasure is found, reclassify Treasure as morale, or characterize divisible wealth as sentimental rubble.

### 11.3 No Opportunistic Concealment
The Burglar shall not conceal substantial recoveries for the purpose of coercing renegotiation, except to the limited extent temporary concealment is reasonably necessary to prevent immediate violence or mutual ruin.

### 11.4 Confidential Information
The Burglar shall not disclose confidential route information, secret entrances, keys, maps, passwords, or treasure intelligence to outsiders except where disclosure is reasonably necessary to save life, secure lawful assistance, or prevent a greater wrong.

## Article 12. Breach and Termination

### 12.1 Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate this Agreement for Material Breach upon reasonable notice and a reasonable opportunity to cure, unless the breach by its nature cannot be cured.

### 12.2 Immediate Termination
The Company may terminate immediately for deliberate betrayal by the Burglar. The Burglar may terminate immediately for deliberate abandonment, material fraud, or demand of plainly unlawful or suicidal performance not contemplated by this Agreement.

### 12.3 Termination by Mutual Agreement
The parties may terminate this Agreement at any time by mutual written consent.

### 12.4 Effect of Termination
Termination shall not affect accrued rights, including rights to payment, reimbursement, indemnity, restitution, reliance damages, or dispute resolution.

## Article 13. Remedies

### 13.1 General Remedies
A non-breaching party shall be entitled to all remedies available at law or in equity, including damages, restitution, specific relief where appropriate, and declaratory relief.

### 13.2 Liquidated Damages for Wrongful Exclusion
If the Company, after substantial performance by the Burglar, wrongfully excludes him from the final division of Treasure, the Burglar shall be entitled to liquidated damages equal to the greater of:

(a) one-fourteenth of the fair appraised value of recoverable Treasure actually secured; or
(b) three times the Burglar's unreimbursed expenditures.

The parties acknowledge that actual damages in such circumstances may be difficult to calculate and that this clause is intended as a reasonable pre-estimate and not as a penalty, however emotionally deserved a penalty might be.

### 13.3 Mitigation
An injured party shall use reasonable efforts to mitigate damages, but no party shall be required to mitigate by marching unarmed into a dragon's mouth or by entrusting treasure division to plainly interested parties.

## Article 14. Assignment, Delegation, and Third Parties

### 14.1 No Assignment by Burglar of Personal Services
The Burglar may not assign or delegate his core personal-services duties without the Company's prior written consent.

### 14.2 No Substitution by Company
The Company may not substitute another person, beast, or wizard as the Burglar without the Burglar's written consent.

### 14.3 Third-Party Beneficiaries
Except for the Burglar's heirs and estate with respect to accrued monetary rights, this Agreement creates no enforceable rights in any third party, and no dragon shall claim status as an incidental beneficiary merely because treasure remains near him.

## Article 15. General Provisions

### 15.1 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement of the parties concerning the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior oral or written discussions, songs, boasts, assurances, or strategically vague wizardly remarks.

### 15.2 Amendments
No amendment or waiver shall be effective unless in writing and signed by both parties, except that an oral modification made in mortal peril may be enforceable to the extent fully performed and clearly proven by disinterested witnesses.

### 15.3 Interpretation
Headings are for convenience only. Ambiguities shall be interpreted in light of the Agreement's purpose and, where appropriate, against the drafter of the disputed language.

### 15.4 Severability
If any provision is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in effect to the fullest extent permitted, and the invalid provision shall be reformed if possible to approximate its lawful intent.

### 15.5 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by the customs of the Shire, supplemented by the common law of the Free Peoples and equitable principles suitable to civilized folk.

### 15.6 Dispute Resolution
Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall first be submitted to mediation before a neutral person of sound judgment. If mediation fails, the dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration before a panel of three: one hobbit, one dwarf, and one neutral elf, each selected by fair means and with some reluctance.

### 15.7 Venue
Any in-person proceeding shall occur in a location reasonably accessible, substantially defensible, and, to the extent practicable, free from dragons.

### 15.8 Notices
Any notice required under this Agreement shall be given by hand, trusted messenger, post, raven, or other means reasonably calculated to reach the recipient. Notices shouted into the wind shall be effective only upon actual receipt.

### 15.9 Counterparts
This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one instrument.

### 15.10 Survival
Articles concerning payment, indemnity, restitution, reliance, remedies, confidentiality, dispute resolution, and interpretation shall survive expiration or termination of this Agreement.
